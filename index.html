Lore of Ishikawa Goemon

石川五右衛門 (Ishikawa Goemon)
The Legendary Phantom Thief (1558-1594)
Born during Japan's tumultuous Sengoku period, Goemon became the most famous outlaw in Japanese history. Operating during the Azuchi-Momoyama period, he led a band of thieves who stole from corrupt feudal lords and wealthy merchants, redistributing their wealth to suffering peasants. His legend transcended death when he was executed by being boiled alive on orders of Toyotomi Hideyoshi, becoming an eternal symbol of rebellion against tyrannical authority.
